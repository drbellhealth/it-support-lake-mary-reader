Managed Services & Co-Managed IT

For a Lake Mary finance firm, the managed-services agreement is built to keep tax and accounting systems available and secure, especially when a filing deadline turns every hour into money. The provider monitors the servers, workstations, and client-portal infrastructure the practice runs on, and acts on warnings before a crash during a crunch. Dytech Group offers month-to-month options, which a firm can weigh against the cost of a system outage in April. The scope conversation should confirm how client financial data is protected and which accounting and tax applications fall inside the agreement.

US-Based Help Desk & End-User Support

At a finance firm, a help desk call during filing season is an emergency in miniature — a tax package that will not open a return, a client portal that will not accept an upload, a printer jammed before a deadline. Dytech Group runs a US-based help desk whose technicians understand accounting software and the sensitivity of financial data, so a Lake Mary CPA or bookkeeper reaches someone who grasps the deadline pressure. Routine issues resolve remotely; anything blocking a filing gets the priority the calendar demands.

Cybersecurity, EDR & SOC Coverage

Financial firms are prime targets because they hold concentrated, monetizable data — Social Security numbers, account details, full financial pictures — which raises the stakes for a Lake Mary practice. A provider layers defenses tuned to a finance office: endpoint detection and encryption on every workstation, email filtering to stop the phishing and wire-fraud attempts that target accounting staff, multi-factor authentication on tax software and client portals, and ongoing monitoring. Because a breach of client financial data carries regulatory and reputational fallout, tested backups and a written incident plan are essential rather than optional.

Cloud, Microsoft 365 & VoIP

Accounting practices increasingly run tax and bookkeeping software and client portals from the cloud so staff and clients exchange documents securely from anywhere, and a provider helps a Lake Mary firm choose what to host, migrate it safely, and secure access. On telephony, VoIP brings call routing and softphones useful when staff work extended hours in season, but financial conversations and document exchange demand reliable connectivity, so bandwidth assessment and a backup path belong in any responsible plan. Secure handling of client documents shapes how portals and messaging are configured.

What Onboarding Looks Like

Onboarding a Lake Mary finance firm begins with discovery focused on where financial data lives and how exposed it is: the provider inventories the tax and accounting servers, finance workstations, and the backups protecting client records, and verifies whether those backups have been restored. That audit often surfaces a firm's real risks — an unencrypted laptop with client returns, a tax server past warranty, or portal access that is too broad. From there the provider deploys monitoring, encryption, and Safeguards-aligned controls, documents the environment, and sets up the support channels staff will use.
